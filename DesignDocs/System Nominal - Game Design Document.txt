Game Design Document: System Nominal (Working Title)
1. Overview
Logline: Assume the captain's chair in a living, dynamic solar system governed by N-body gravity. Trade, fight, and navigate complex orbital paths to earn enough Fame to become a legend before your fuel—or your luck—runs out.
Genre: Orbital Physics RPG / Sandbox / Trading Simulator
Inspirations: Xia: Legends of a Drift System, Kerbal Space Program, The Expanse
Theme: Hard Sci-fi, Frontier Capitalism, Gravitational Mastery
2. Core Gameplay Loop
1. Plot & Burn: Players use a Nav-Computer to plot trajectories between moving bodies. Navigation requires timed burns to alter orbits while balancing acceleration ($a$) against current ship mass ($m$) and fuel.
2. Execute: Transition to a tactical bridge view to execute maneuvers. Dock with rotating stations, mine asteroids, or engage in "Relative-Frame" combat.
3. Earn & Upgrade: Convert cargo and bounties into credits. Install more efficient engines, larger fuel tanks, or high-output reactors to handle more demanding orbits.
4. Ascend: Accumulate 20 Fame Points through legendary deeds to win.
3. Win & Loss Conditions
* Victory: Reach the 20 Fame threshold to enter the "Legacy" screen, summarizing your career.
* Defeat: * Hull Breach: Integrity reaches zero from combat or collision.
   * The Dead Orbit: Running out of fuel with no intersecting path to a station.
   * Rescue: Broadcast a "Mayday" to be towed to the nearest station at the cost of 75% credits and 2 Fame.
4. Physics: The $F = ma$ Engine
The game operates on a persistent Newtonian simulation where momentum is never lost to "friction."
* N-Body Gravity: All 10 planets, their moons, and the Sun (Solara) exert gravitational pull on the ship simultaneously.
* Variable Acceleration: As fuel is consumed, the ship’s mass decreases, making the same thrust ($F$) produce higher acceleration ($a$).
* The Sun (Solara): The dominant gravity well. It enables the Oberth Effect, where burns performed deep in the Sun's well are significantly more efficient for high-velocity travel.
* Trajectory Prediction: The HUD renders "Ghost Paths"—dynamic orbital projections that shift in real-time as the player applies thrust.

	6. The Solara System Layout
6.1 Inner Zone (Rocky/High-Security)
* Solara: The central star. Source of gravity and solar radiation.
* Ignis: Tidally locked cinder. Produces Rare Metals. Extreme heat hazard.
* The Foundry: Industrial hub. Produces Machinery.
* Aethelgard: Capital world. High demand for Luxuries. Home to the Vane shipyard.
* Gaea Prime: Terraformed garden. Produces Food Paks and Medicine.
6.2 The Belt of Tears
* A massive ring of asteroids between the Inner and Outer zones. Home to Ceres Prime (Trade Hub) and pirate hideouts.
6.3 Outer Zone (Gas Giants/Frontier)
* Kronos: Primary source of Helium-3 (Fuel). Moons: Hyperion (Agri) and Atlas (Refinery).
* Oceanus: Ice giant. Produces Water/Oxygen. Moon: Triton (Military).
* Erebus: Stormy giant. Produces Advanced Electronics. Moon: Nyx (Black Market).
* Caelus: Ringed ice giant. Source of Rare Crystals. Moon: Ariel (Science).
* Tartarus: Dwarf planet on an eccentric orbit. Source of Illegal Stims.
* Solara-B: Brown dwarf at the edge. Home to the Void-Gate derelict.
7. Combat & Flight Assist (Arcade Style)
To bridge the gap between "Hard Physics" and "Fun Gameplay," the ship features a Combat Flight Computer.
7.1 Pursuit Assist (The Arcade Toggle)
When a target is locked, the ship enters Relative Frame Mode:
* Velocity Matching: The computer automatically burns thrusters to match the target's orbital velocity. This makes the enemy appear "still" or "dogfightable" relative to the player.
* The Cost: Maintaining this "Arcade Pocket" consumes fuel constantly. Fighting a ship in a wildly different orbit (e.g., trying to intercept a moon-bound ship while in a solar orbit) will drain tanks rapidly.
* Point-and-Shoot: While Assist is active, WASD controls provide intuitive "arcade" movement (strafe, forward, back) relative to the target.
7.2 Tactical Weaponry
* Auto-Cannons (Kinetic): Projectiles inherit the ship's velocity. Best for "jousting" passes.
* Pulse Lasers (Energy): Hit-scan at close range. Drains ship Power and generates Heat.
* Torpedoes: Self-propelled AI craft that use their own $F=ma$ logic to intercept the enemy's path.
8. Tactical Systems: Heat & Power
* Heat: Engines and weapons generate thermal energy. If not dissipated via Radiators, the ship takes hull damage. Proximity to Solara increases passive heat gain.
* Power: Reactors provide the energy for Ion engines and Shields. Solar Panels are effective in the Inner Zone but become useless beyond the Belt.
